Summary of: Development of grouping principles

In the upcoming Oxford Handbook of Perceptual Organization from our lab, Quinn & Bhatt (2013) discuss how the developmental trajectory of grouping principles. Here I summarize key findings.

General idea: Certain grouping principles might be innate while the rest develop quickly though possibly at different time courses.

Innate core principles:

  • Common movement (Kellman & Spelke, 1983)
  • Connected surface (Spelke, 1982)

Learned by 3-4 months (using the core principles via attentional enhancement and group persistency (unitization) mechanisms, see Quinn & Bhatt, 2009, Quinn & Bhatt, 2011; Brunswik & Kamiya, 1953):

  • Proximity (Quinn, Bhatt, & Hayden, 2008)
  • Good continuation (Quinn & Bhatt, 2005a)
  • Similarity:
    • Surface
    • Lightness (Quinn, Burke, & Rush, 1993)
  • Subjective contours (Kanizsa figures; Ghim, 1990)
  • Global shape processing priority (Ghim & Eimas, 1988)
  • Connectedness (note that this may be identical to the connected surface principle; Hayden, Bhatt, & Quinn, 2006)
  • Common region (Bhatt, Hayden, & Quinn, 2007; Hayden et al., 2008)
  • Transfer:
    • Connectedness -> Proximity (Kangas et al., 2011)

Learned by 6-7 months (but accessible by 3-4 months, see Quinn & Bhatt, 2005b):

  • Form similarity (Quinn et al., 2002)
  • Transfer:
    • Lightness similarity -> Form similarity (Quinn & Bhatt, 2009)
    • Common region -> Proximity (Kangas et al., 2011)
  • Salience hierarchy: Lightness similarity > Connectedness > Form similarity (Hayden et al., 2009)

Development continues into adolescence…
